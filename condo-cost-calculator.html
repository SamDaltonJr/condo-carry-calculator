Reading it: hatched segments are money that leaves you each month (interest, tax, insurance, HOA, PMI). The solid green slice is the principal you keep. True monthly cost nets that equity back out — it's the closest thing to "rent equivalent" for owning. The forgone return stat is the opportunity cost of parking cash in the down payment instead of the market. Property tax shown is fixed-rate on today's price; in reality assessed value (and thus tax) tends to rise over time.
